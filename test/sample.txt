The three tables illustrate changes in the total population of New York City from 1800 to 2000, broken down by Manhattan and the other four districts.
Overall, the population of New York City experienced exponential growth. Although Manhattan accounted for the vast majority of the city's residents in 1800, the other districts surpassed it by 2000, becoming the dominant demographic force.
The total population of New York City witnessed a dramatic rise, growing from approximately 79,000 in 1800 to over 8 million in 2000. This represents a hundred-fold increase over the two-century period.
In terms of distribution, both Manhattan and the other districts saw significant numerical growth between 1800 and 1900. However, Manhattan's share of the total population declined steadily, dropping from 76% to a low of 19% in 2000. In contrast, the other districts showed remarkable expansion. Their proportion of the total population rose consistently, nearly doubling between 1800 and 1900, before peaking at 81% in 2000.
